Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about migrating from Kentico 13 to Xperience by Kentico.

Kentico 13 official support ends on December 31, 2026. After the end-of-support deadline, businesses will no longer receive Kentico security updates, bug fixes, or technical support. Upgrading to Xperience by Kentico helps enterprises improve security, scalability, performance, and long-term digital experience platform support.

After Kentico 13 end of support, websites will no longer receive official security updates, bug fixes, or technical support. Businesses using unsupported Kentico CMS versions may face higher security risks, compliance challenges, performance issues, and increased maintenance costs compared to upgrading to Xperience by Kentico or modern enterprise CMS platforms.

The complexity of a Kentico migration depends on custom modules, API integrations, workflows, legacy architecture, and content structure. A structured Xperience by Kentico migration roadmap helps businesses modernize enterprise CMS infrastructure, reduce migration risks, and move to a scalable digital experience platform with minimal downtime.

Before starting a Kentico upgrade, businesses should review custom modules, API integrations, content architecture, hosting environments, SEO structure, security requirements, and cloud infrastructure. A Kentico migration assessment helps identify redevelopment needs, technical gaps, and opportunities for enterprise digital transformation and cloud modernization.

Modern digital experience platforms provide better scalability, personalization, cloud readiness, API integrations, security, and omnichannel content management compared to traditional CMS platforms like WordPress and legacy enterprise systems. Businesses are increasingly adopting composable DXP and headless CMS architecture to support scalable digital experiences, AI integration, and enterprise growth.

A traditional CMS manages content and frontend presentation together, while a headless CMS separates content management from frontend delivery using APIs. Headless CMS platforms like Contentful and Contentstack provide greater flexibility for omnichannel experiences, enterprise integrations, scalable web applications, and modern digital product development.

Common enterprise CMS migration challenges include legacy integrations, outdated custom modules, SEO preservation, content restructuring, deployment downtime, and infrastructure modernization. Working with experienced Kentico migration experts and enterprise CMS consultants helps businesses reduce technical debt and ensure smoother digital platform upgrades.

Enterprise businesses use AI for automation, intelligent search, analytics, workflow optimization, customer support, and personalized digital experiences. Technologies like Microsoft Copilot, Azure OpenAI, AI chatbots, and enterprise AI automation help organizations improve productivity, operational efficiency, and customer engagement across digital platforms.

Composable DXP architecture allows businesses to combine best-of-breed platforms, APIs, cloud services, and headless CMS solutions instead of relying on monolithic CMS systems. This approach improves scalability, personalization, flexibility, integration capabilities, and long-term adaptability for enterprise digital experience platform modernization.
